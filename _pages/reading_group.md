The Lausanne Theory for Empirical Economics Reading Group and Seminar
========

This reading group is tailored for PhD students interested in developing their skills in theoretical methods that could be relevant to their work (examples include contract theory or quantitative spatial economics for development economics, structural models for political economy).
Each week, for one hour, PhD students discuss a paper or one or two PhD student present their research. 

If you are interested to join the reading group, to present or to be part of the mailing list, please send me an email at julian.marcoux@unil.ch. 

Approximately once per month, the Lausanne Theory for Empirical Economics Reading Group and Seminar joins the [Rare Voices Micro Research Cluster](https://www.rarevoicesineconomics.com/research-clusters) to attend presentations by PhD students from the University of Lausanne, the University of Geneva, and the Graduate Institute. For 1.5 hours, one or two PhD students present their research in a seminar-style setting and receive feedback.

                                                                                                                                                   
The reading group take place in Internef XX, but a Zoom link is provided to allow external PhD students to join and participate.
